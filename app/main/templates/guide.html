Adding Journal Entries Guide

Welcome to the guide on how to add journal entries on MindCanvas journaling app! Start by clicking on the button from your dashboard. This will direct you to a page featuring a rich text editor, where you can compose your journal entry (as demonstrated below).

Step 1: Entering Your Daily Reflections

Begin by typing your thoughts, reflections, or any narrative you wish to include in your journal entry.

To include mathematical equations, utilize the MathJax integration within the text editor. Simply enclose your mathematical expressions within appropriate MathJax delimiters. For example,

Today, I explored the fascinating world of prime numbers. One of the most intriguing properties of primes is their distribution, as exemplified by the Prime Number Theorem: \[ \pi(x) \sim \frac{x}{\log(x)} \] This theorem elegantly describes the density of prime numbers up to a given value of \(x\). Within the text, you can include inline equations by enclosing them within a pair of `\(` and `\)` symbols, like so: \( E = mc^2 \).

Preview after Rendering

Here's how your text will look after rendering:

Today, I explored the fascinating world of prime numbers. One of the most intriguing properties of primes is their distribution, as exemplified by the Prime Number Theorem:

\( \pi(x) \sim \frac{x}{\log(x)} \)

This theorem elegantly describes the density of prime numbers up to a given value of \(x\).

Within the text, you can include inline equations by enclosing them within a pair of `\(` and `\)` symbols, like so: \( E = mc^2 \).

Step 2: Review and Add

Once you've composed your entry, take a moment to review it for any errors or modifications. Once satisfied, hit the button to add your journal entry.
